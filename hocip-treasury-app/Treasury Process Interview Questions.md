Treasury Process Interview Questions
AS-IS Mapping
AFM Dzingidzingi Church Of SA — Treasury Process Digitisation

The following questions are designed to understand the current (AS-IS) treasury processes at AFM Dzingidzingi Church Of SA. Your responses will be used to design and develop a digital financial recording system tailored to your needs. There are no right or wrong answers — please respond as openly and honestly as possible.
1. Overview of Treasury Process

•	Can you walk me through what happens from when money is received until it is recorded?
-	After when money is received it is counted and verified by the treasurer. Thereafter, the money is recorded in an exercise book with coulumns labelled as follows: Date, Receipt number, Names (from where the money is), Total amount received, Tithes, Offerings, Pledges and bank interest. This is done every time there is church service where money is collected. At the end of the month we close our financial books manually. The same process is repeated for the following month until the end of the financial year. At the end of the financial year, our financial books are audited and we often find that our books are not balanced due to addition errors. 


•	What types of money do you handle (e.g. tithes, offerings, donations)?
-	Tithes, offerings, pledges and bank inerest


•	How often do you handle or record transactions?
-	Four times per month or every time there is an income


2. Money Collection Process

•	How is money collected during services?
-	People put their money in the busket


•	Who is involved in collecting the money?
-	The treasurer is responsible in collecting the money 


•	When does the counting take place?
-	After church service


•	Is the money counted by more than one person?
-	Yes, two people do the counting, the first counts and the second verifies


•	Is there any verification or double-checking process?
-	Yes, the first person counts and the second person double check as a way of verification


3. Recording Process

•	How do you currently record the money?
-	It is recorded in an esrcise book with columns drawn by hands with titles


•	Do you use a physical book, Excel, or another method?
-	I use a physical book with columns and rows dramn by hands


•	What details do you capture when recording (e.g. date, amount, type)?
-	Date, Name of the person making a contribution, total amount paid. Tithes, offerings,pledges and bank ineterests

•	Who is responsible for recording the transactions?
-	The treasurer


•	When is the recording done (immediately, after service, later)?
-	Immediately after the service



4. Categorisation of Funds

•	How do you separate tithes, offerings, and donations?
-	I use columns with  relevant titles.


•	Are these recorded separately or together?
-	They are recorded together on one page under specific columns


•	Do you track who contributed, or only totals?
-	Yes, we track them since they write their names in rows and record their money as per the columns.  


5. Expense Management

•	How do you record expenses?
-	Expenses are a recorded in the same physical book as the income, but on pages designated as expenditure with columns titled date, purpose/for, bank charges and amount used.


•	Who is responsible for approving expenses?
-	The pastor and the treasurer


•	How do you ensure expenses do not exceed available funds?
-	Before an expenditure is approve, we chech whether the funds are available


•	Are expenses linked to specific categories or purposes?
-	Yes, we have monthly expenses like salaries or stipends. Church maintainance and other general expenses


6. Storage and Security

•	Where is the money stored after counting?
-	The money is stored in a locked safe overnight and it is then taken to the bank the following day 


•	Who has access to the stored money?
-	The treasurer 


•	Are there any controls to prevent errors or loss?
-	The money is stored in a locked safe to prevent any loss


•	How do you ensure accountability?
-	Financial books are audited by a professional auditor 


7. Reporting Process

•	What types of reports do you prepare?
-	Monthly reports and end of the year report


•	How often do you prepare reports (weekly, monthly, yearly)?
-	Monthly and yearly


•	Who do you report to?
-	The treasurer


•	How are these reports created?
-	They are created manually


•	How long does it take to prepare reports?
-	It takes me three weeks to prepare the end of the year report


8. Record Keeping and History

•	How do you store past financial records?
-	They are stored as hard copies by the treasurer since I record our records in a physical book


•	Are records stored physically, digitally, or both?
-	Physically


•	How easy is it to retrieve old records?

•	Have records ever been lost, damaged, or difficult to access?

13. Readiness for a Digital System

•	What would make a system easy for you to use?
